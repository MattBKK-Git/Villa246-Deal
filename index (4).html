Villa 246 — Investment Opportunity

The Residence Cherng Talay, Phuket

Background

John is a long-term British holiday-home owner who has owned a villa at The Residence Cherng Talay, Phuket for the last 21 years. The structure is the standard Thai one: John holds a 30-year lease on the villa (building + land use), and the underlying land is owned by the developer, who issued the 30-year lease.

In breach of the agreement with John, the developer borrowed money against the land. The developer has since defaulted with the lender, and the land has been seized by the Thai courts. A public auction is scheduled for 23 July 2026 so the lender can recover their money. John's lease remains active for another 9 years.

Through a long-standing relationship with John, we have been in a unique position to gather significant information around this opportunity, and have been able to negotiate a very favourable deal with John due to his age and needs around the villa.

The opportunity

We will acquire the freehold land at auction and become John's new landlord. As part of the deal, John will continue using the villa under his current lease for a fixed period of 3 years, at which time the lease transfers to us — giving us full control of both the freehold land and the villa, completely clear of the developer. At that point the villa is renovated and sold at an estimated ฿33M.

Rental context: the villa currently rents at approximately ฿300,000/month during the 8-week December–January high season, and ฿130,000–150,000/month for the remainder of the year.

Background on John

  • John is 86 years old, UK-based
  • Currently uses the villa twice a year, 1–2 months each visit, and wants to continue to do so for the next 3 years
  • Has agreed to use the villa for a fixed 3-year period and exit with a fixed payout
  • His existing 30-year lease is held by his company and has 9 years remaining (expires January 2036), which protects John from eviction after the auction

Why we can win this bid

The 9 years remaining on John's lease makes the property effectively frozen for any buyer without a relationship with him. A stranger would have to sit on it for 9 years with no ability to use, renovate, or resell it. Our history with John has changed that equation. John, now 86, would rather take a fair lump-sum payout that reflects his position in the villa and remaining time on the lease than face the alternative: a forced handover of his villa back to the developer at the end of the 9-year period. That relationship, and that alignment of interests, is what turns a 9-year encumbrance into a 3-year opportunity.

The strategy

  1. Acquire the freehold land through auction (or pre-auction settlement).
  2. Lease transfers to us at the end of the 3-year fixed period. We already own and control the land from the auction; once the lease also transfers, we have full control of the entire property — land, lease and building. All transfer documentation is pre-executed at his company level upfront — the same company that holds the lease — so nothing can be reversed or changed on John's side.
  3. Renovate and sell within approximately 6 months of the lease transfer. A targeted modernisation — an additional bedroom, updated finishes and new furniture — to maximise sale value. Target sale price ฿33–35M.

Fixed timeframe: 3 years to lease transfer, ~6 months to sale.

Your potential investment

You contribute ฿1,800,000 up front to help fund the land acquisition, plus a further ฿1,125,000 at the end of the fixed 3-year period to help fund the renovation and prepare the villa for sale.

Total commitment from you: ฿2,925,000

Note: Your funds are only required if we are successful at auction. If we're not, no capital is called.

Your return (2.5x your money in 3 years)

You take a share of the net profit when the villa sells. On a base case sale of ฿33M, your total back is ฿7,312,500 after all costs have been paid — including legals, transfer fees, taxes and all other costs. This includes your ฿2,925,000 principal returned.
